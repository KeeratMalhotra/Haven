## The opener (paste this first)

Most productivity tools are passive filing cabinets: they store your tasks and remind you of deadlines, but they never actually *help you act*. Haven is different. It's an AI companion that plans your day, watches your commitments, and speaks up **before** a deadline is missed — then learns how you actually work so every week gets easier.

Tell Haven your week in plain words and it builds your tasks, calendar, and habits instantly. Then it goes further than any reminder app: it notices when you've overcommitted, warns you when a deadline won't fit the time you have left, and offers a one-tap fix — **without being asked.** And it remembers. Haven learns the hours you actually do deep work, the tasks you keep pushing, and the rhythm of your days, then plans around the real you.

That's the leap from a to-do list to a companion — and it's why Haven is a genuine "Last-Minute Life Saver," not another notification you'll ignore.

---

## What makes Haven different (the moat)

1. **It's proactive, not passive.** A dedicated intelligence engine runs continuously with five conservative detectors — overcommitment, deadline trajectory, repeated-reschedule patterns, recovery, and protective buffers — and surfaces help *before* things break, governed so it's respectful (frequency budget, never during focus, learned calibration).
2. **It learns you.** Persistent behavioral memory distills how you actually work — productive hours, estimate accuracy, patterns you keep slipping — and feeds that back into planning. The more you use it, the smarter it gets about *your* rhythm. That's compounding value a competitor can't copy by cloning a screen.
3. **It takes action, end to end.** Native Google Calendar, Tasks, Gmail, and Slides integration means Haven doesn't just suggest — it schedules, completes, drafts, and builds. Auto-Pilot can plan an entire day autonomously around your real commitments.
4. **It's production-grade, not a prototype.** Multi-agent architecture, graceful degradation on every AI call, security hardening, and a live deployment on Google Cloud Run.
