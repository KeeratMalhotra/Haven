## Research endpoint: disclaimer without structural fix

The prompt still instructs Gemini to use "real, well-known websites like MDN, Wikipedia, Stack Overflow" — which produces URLs that *look* real but aren't verified to exist. A link to `https://developer.mozilla.org/en-US/docs/Web/API/SomeFeatureThatDoesntExist` is more confusing than obviously fake because the domain is trusted.

Two mitigation options without major architecture changes: (1) strip `source_url` from results entirely and present the research as text-only analysis, or (2) use Gemini's `google_search_retrieval` tool which returns grounded results with real URLs.

## Email content in AI prompts

The Gmail scan endpoint concatenates up to 15 email bodies (500 chars each) directly into the Gemini prompt:

```python
f"Email {i+1}:\n  ID: {e['id']}\n  From: {e['from']}\n  Subject: {e['subject']}\n  Date: {e['date']}\n  Preview: {e['body_preview'][:500]}"
```

The body is truncated to 1000 chars in `truncated_body` but only 500 are used in the prompt. The attack surface: any sender whose email lands in the user's inbox can influence the Gemini output. The impact is limited to manipulating which tasks are suggested (not arbitrary code execution), but could trick users into creating tasks with misleading titles or notes.
